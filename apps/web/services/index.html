Honest, in advance

Pricing, no surprises.

I'd rather you knew the numbers before you enquired than felt awkward asking. Here's how I price, what a deposit covers, and what comes as standard every time.

What it costs

Rates & sessions.

Minimum charge

£80

Even the smallest tattoo needs a sterile setup, a fresh station and drawing time, so there's a floor. It covers little fine-line pieces, up to about an hour in the chair.

Small bespoke (under 2 hours)

£120–£200

A single, self-contained piece: a delicate sprig, a small animal, a bit of script. Priced as a flat rate once I've seen the idea, so you know the cost before we book.

Half day (3–4 hours)

£300

For a larger single piece, or a few smaller ones in one sitting. Design time, stencil and aftercare guidance are all included.

Full day (6–7 hours)

£500

A full session for bigger or more detailed work. There's no clock-watching: we take the breaks we need and finish when it's right.

Touch-up (within a year)

Free

Skin is unpredictable, and now and then a spot wants a little attention as it settles. I'll check in on how it's healing, and if you feel it needs one, you can ask for a free touch-up within a year, where the aftercare's been followed.

Deposits & cancellations

Booking & deposits.

Deposit 50% of total
Reschedule window 48h+
No-show Deposit forfeit
Balance Due on the day
Payment Card or bank

A 50% deposit secures the date and the time I spend drawing your design, and it comes off the final price on the day. Give me at least 48 hours to move an appointment and it carries over. Miss it without notice and the deposit covers the lost slot and design work.

The detail

Cover-ups & touch-ups.

Cover-ups

Covering old work.

Cover-ups and reworks aren't my favourite job, but they're one I'm good at, and they're their own puzzle. What's possible depends on how dark, how big and how old the existing tattoo is, so I'll always want a clear, well-lit photo before I say yes.

They usually need a bolder, larger design than a fresh piece, and they're priced by session rather than as a flat rate, since they take longer to plan and place.

Send a photo with your enquiry and mark it as a cover-up, and I'll tell you honestly whether it's something I can do well.

Touch-ups

Keeping it crisp.

One free touch-up comes with every piece, within a year, as long as the aftercare's been followed.

I'll check in on how it's settling, and if you feel a spot wants attention, just message me and we'll find a short slot. Touch-ups are quick and booked around the bigger work.

What's not covered: heavy fading from sun or picking, or wanting to change the design later. That's a fresh booking.
