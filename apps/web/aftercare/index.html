Post-session

Aftercare.

How you look after your tattoo in the first few weeks shapes how it heals for the rest of your life. Start by telling us how it was wrapped. Your steps appear below.

Step one

How was your tattoo wrapped?

Your routine depends on the dressing you left the studio with. Pick the one that matches and your step-by-step appears below. You can switch any time.

Not sure which you have? Whatever is on your skin right now is your answer, or ask us.

Your routine

Second skin

Transparent
film dressing.

Second skin (also called Saniderm or Dermalize) is a breathable, waterproof film applied directly after the session. It creates a sealed healing environment and reduces scabbing.

  1. 01

    Keep the bandage on for 2–3 days after your tattoo session.

    Some plasma and ink pooling under the film is completely normal during this time. Don't be alarmed.

  2. 02

    Wash your hands thoroughly with antibacterial soap.

  3. 03

    Gently peel off the bandage under running water, pulling upward and away from the tattoo.

    Running water loosens the adhesive. Never pull the film dry or at an angle across the tattoo.

  4. 04

    Lather a non-scented soap in your hands and gently clean the tattoo to remove plasma and debris.

    Use your fingertips only: no cloths, sponges, or anything with texture.

  5. 05

    Rinse with lukewarm (not hot) water and pat dry with a clean paper towel.

  6. 06

    Let the tattoo air dry for 20 minutes, then apply a thin layer of tattoo aftercare moisturiser. We recommend Palmer's Cocoa Butter.

    Thin is key: too much product suffocates the skin and slows healing.

  7. 07

    Repeat this process twice daily for 2–3 weeks.

  8. 08

    Continue moisturising regularly after healing to maintain vibrancy.

Cling film

Traditional
wrap method.

Cling film is a short-term protective wrap applied after the session. Unlike second skin, it needs to be removed promptly. Leaving it too long traps moisture and increases infection risk.

  1. 01

    When you are ready to apply aftercare, gently remove the cling film as soon as possible.

    Do not leave cling film on for more than a few hours. It doesn't breathe and will trap bacteria.

  2. 02

    Wash your hands thoroughly with antibacterial soap.

  3. 03

    Lather a non-scented soap in your hands and gently clean the tattoo to remove plasma and debris.

    Use your fingertips only: no cloths, sponges, or anything with texture.

  4. 04

    Rinse with lukewarm (not hot) water and pat dry with a clean paper towel.

  5. 05

    Let the tattoo air dry for 20 minutes, then apply a thin layer of tattoo aftercare moisturiser. We recommend Palmer's Cocoa Butter.

    Thin is key: too much product suffocates the skin and slows healing.

  6. 06

    Repeat this process twice daily for 2–4 weeks.

  7. 07

    Continue moisturising regularly after healing to maintain vibrancy.

Both methods

Keep in mind.

No soaking

Avoid baths, swimming, saunas, and hot tubs for 2–3 weeks. Showers are fine. Just keep them short and the water lukewarm.

Don't pick or scratch

Peeling and itching is normal during healing. Picking removes ink and can cause scarring. Let it flake on its own.

Avoid direct sunlight

Sun and UV beds fade ink rapidly during healing. Keep the area covered or out of direct sun for the first 4 weeks. SPF 50 after that, always.
