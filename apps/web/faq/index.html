Send whatever you have: a few reference images, a rough placement, a mood. Part of my job is helping you shape it into something that works on skin. We'll go back and forth until it feels right, before anything's booked.

Honestly, a bit — but it's very manageable, and almost everyone says it's easier than they'd built it up to be. It depends on the spot and on the day. We go at your pace, take breaks whenever you need them, and there's no prize for toughing it out. I'll talk you through what to expect before we start.

Eat a proper meal beforehand, drink plenty of water and get a good night's sleep. Wear comfy clothes that give easy access to the area, and bring a snack and a drink for longer sessions. Skip alcohol the night before, and don't turn up with fresh sunburn where you're being tattooed.

One person is welcome, especially if it helps you feel settled — just bear in mind the room is small, so it's one guest rather than a group. For longer sessions some people prefer to come solo and put their headphones in. Whatever's most comfortable for you.

I don't tattoo during pregnancy or while breastfeeding, for your safety and the baby's. If you're on medication or managing a health condition, just tell me in your enquiry — most things are completely fine, but a few need a doctor's note or a little planning, and some skin conditions we'd want to work around. Nothing you share goes any further.

Come in, get comfortable, and we'll go over the design and placement together. I'll get a stencil on, you check the position in the mirror, and we don't start until you're happy. There's a quick consent form and a chat about aftercare too.

On the day, before we start. I draw each piece custom and like to keep it fresh, so you'll see it in person rather than weeks ahead. There's always room for small adjustments to size, placement or detail before the stencil goes on.

Small ones, yes — sizing, placement and little details are all part of the process. Bigger changes, like a totally different subject or style, usually mean redrawing and might need a separate session. If you're unsure about anything, tell me before we start rather than partway through.

The surface heals in about two to three weeks; the deeper layers settle over a couple of months. Everyone's a little different, and that's normal. See the aftercare page for the full step-by-step guide.

Second skin is a breathable adhesive film that stays on for a few days and protects the fresh tattoo through its early healing; cling film is just a short-term cover for the journey home. I'll usually send you off in second skin and show you how to take it off. See the full aftercare guide.

It depends on size, detail and placement. There's a minimum charge for small pieces, flat rates for small custom work, and half- or full-day rates for bigger pieces — and I'll always confirm the cost before we book. Full pricing on the services page.

A deposit secures your date and covers the time I spend drawing your design. It comes off the final price on the day. Give me at least 72 hours' notice to move an appointment and it carries over; miss it without notice and it's forfeited.

Life happens — just give me as much notice as you can. With 72 hours or more, I'll move you to a new date and your deposit carries over. Less than that, or a no-show, means the deposit's used up and a new one's needed to rebook.
