The artist · Winchester

Hi, I'm Roxy.

I'm the hands and the name behind Beansprout — a fine line, botanical and illustrative tattooer working out of Tiny Knives in Winchester. I draw custom, one client at a time, at a pace that never feels rushed.

Nice to meet you

Tattoos that feel like yours.

I've always been the person friends came to when they wanted something drawn, and tattooing turned out to be the most honest version of that. Every piece I make starts with a conversation rather than a flash sheet, and ends as something that belongs to one person only.

My work leans fine line and botanical — flowers, ferns, the small details that make a piece feel grown rather than stamped on — but I'm just as happy deep in an illustrative piece: animals, characters, the things you grew up loving. I'll always tell you honestly whether an idea will sit well on skin, and how it'll age, before we commit to anything.

Mostly, I want the chair to feel calm. Being tattooed is a strange, quietly vulnerable few hours, and I take that seriously — we go at your pace, with breaks whenever you need them, and no pressure to be anyone other than yourself. People tend to leave a little looser than they arrived.

How I work

What I take on, and what I don't.

Where I'm happiest

  • ✓Fine line botanical — flowers, ferns, pressed-flower detail
  • ✓Custom illustrative pieces with a story behind them
  • ✓Animals and pet portraits, realistic or stylised
  • ✓Soft black & grey and gentle dotwork shading
  • ✓Cover-ups and reworks of older tattoos
  • ✓First tattoos — I'll talk you through every step

Not really my thing

  • ×Tribal, lettering-only or large-scale traditional
  • ×Exact copies of another artist's tattoo
  • ×A new partner's name (trust me on this one)
  • ×Fine line too small to heal and age well
  • ×Anything hateful or meant to demean
  • ×Walk-ins — everything is booked and drawn ahead

The space
